Born in South Africa in 1971

Purchased his first computer at age 10. He taught himself how to program.

Made his first software sale—of a game he created called Blastar.

Moved to Canada to attend Queen’s University, but he left in 1992 to study business and physics at the University of Pennsylvania.

He graduated with an undergraduate degree in economics and stayed for a second bachelor’s degree in physics.

Soon after leaving University of Pennsylvania, Elon Musk headed to Stanford University in California to pursue a Ph.D in energy physics and dropped out during the Internet Boom to start launching his first company, Zip2 Corporation.

A division of Compaq Computer Corporation bought Zip2 for $307 million in cash and $34 million in stock options.

Also during this time, Musk co-founded X.com, an online financial services/payments company

X.com got acquired which ked to the creation of PayPal as it is known today.

Got the idea of building spacecraft for commercial space travel and formed Tesla Motors.

SpaceX and Tesla Motors was well established, and NASA awarded SpaceX the contract to handle cargo transport for the International Space Station—with plans for astronaut transport in the future.

PayPal was acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion in stock.

5 years after the formation of Tesla Motors, the company in 2008 unveiled the Roadster, a sports car capable of accelerating from 0 to 60 mph in 3.7 seconds, as well traveling nearly 250 miles between charges of its lithum ion battery.

Musk unveiled a concept for a high-speed transportation system incorporating reduced-pressure tubes in which pressurized capsules ride on an air cushion that would make travel cheaper than any other mode of transport for such long distances.
